# MIGM-Multilingual-Inhibition-L5-Acquisition-Inhibitory-Paradox
The Multilingual Inhibitory Gradient Model (MIGM): Exploring the U-shaped relationship between language proficiency and inhibitory control in higher-order multilingualism (L1-L5).
# The Paradox of Inhibitory Control in Higher-Order Multilingualism
‌
This repository contains the data, visualizations, and theoretical framework for the study: "Why a Dominant L3 May Be Easier to Suppress than a Developing L4 during L5 Acquisition".
‌
## 📌 Project Overview
This research introduces the Multilingual Inhibitory Gradient Model (MIGM) to explain a linguistic paradox: why a highly proficient language (L3) often produces less interference during the acquisition of a new language (L5) than a less-proficient, intermediate language (L4).
‌
### The Core Hypothesis: The Proficiency-Inhibition Paradox
We propose that:
- High Proficiency \rightarrow High Automaticity \rightarrow Efficient Global Inhibition.
- Intermediate Proficiency \rightarrow Active Monitoring \rightarrow Inhibitory Leakage.
‌
## 📊 Key Findings
- L4 (Russian) Interference: 91% of total intrusions.
- L3 (English) Interference: 9% of total intrusions.
- Result: Inhibitory control is more effective in dominant languages, leading to a "U-shaped" relationship between proficiency and interference.
‌
## 📁 Repository Structure
- `/data`: Anonymized datasets of lexical and morphosyntactic intrusions.
- `/visualizations`: Plotting of the MIGM curve and interference distributions.
- `/analysis`: Scripts used for calculating the quantitative disparity between L3 and L4 influence.
